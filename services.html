§ 03 · Beyond the plan

What good planners do that bad planners skip.

Both tiers include these specific deliverables — the kind of thinking that gets quietly forgotten when a “planner” is really a product salesperson.

Value-Add 01

Tax positioning review

Beyond filing — we look at decisions that compound. Roth conversions, Backdoor strategies, capital gains harvesting, charitable bunching.

Value-Add 02

Beneficiary audit

The forgotten document that overrides your will. We check every retirement account, every life insurance policy, every old 401(k) from a former employer.

Value-Add 03

Estate document checklist

Will, healthcare directive, POA, guardianship designation. We don’t draft them — but we make sure none of them is missing or stale.

Value-Add 04

Education funding projection

Multi-child, multi-vehicle (529s, Coverdells, brokerage), realistic about what college will actually cost — and what your family will actually pay.

Value-Add 05

Insurance gap analysis

Term life, disability, umbrella liability, long-term care. The right amount, no more — and exactly when to revisit.

Value-Add 06

Annual cash flow refresh (Ongoing only)

The reset that makes the difference between a plan that ages well and a plan that turns into a relic on your laptop.

§ 04 · Frequently asked

Questions clients ask before they hire.

Why fee-only?

Fee-only means I’m paid by clients, never by product companies. No commissions, no kickbacks, no hidden incentives. The advice you get is the advice that’s best for your family — not the advice that pays me a referral. It’s the only model where the planner’s interests genuinely align with yours.

Do you manage my investments?

No. I’ll recommend an investment approach as part of your plan — what to hold, where, in what ratios — and you’ll implement it through your existing brokerage (Vanguard, Fidelity, Schwab). That keeps fees low and keeps me focused on planning, not on chasing returns.

What if I’m not a homeschool family?

The practice is built for homeschool families and that’s where I do my deepest work. That said, I serve a small number of families outside that niche when there’s clear fit — usually one-income households with similar financial patterns. The discovery call is the right place to decide.

Can I do this if I’m tight on cash flow?

Honest answer: it depends. If you’re in a genuine cash flow crisis, planning isn’t the right first move — budgeting and triage is. If you’re stretched but stable, this is exactly the work that pays back fastest. Discovery call helps us figure out which one you are.

What’s the difference between this and what my insurance agent offers?

An insurance agent sells a product and is paid by commission. A fee-only CFP® sells advice and is paid by you. Both can be useful; they are not the same thing. If your “planner” only recommends products that pay them, that’s not planning.

Do you take clients outside Virginia?

Yes. I’m registered as a fee-only planner and can serve clients in most U.S. states. Confirm your state on the discovery call — there are a small number of jurisdictions where additional registration is required first.

When can we meet?

I work on a surge schedule: concentrated meeting weeks (usually 4–6 weeks per quarter) when client meetings happen, alternating with focus weeks for deep planning work. This is how I keep the quality of every meeting high. Calendly availability reflects this rhythm — if you don’t see a time that works, message me directly.

What do I actually get?

Two documents: the comprehensive written plan (the technical deliverable, usually 40–60 pages) and the One-Page Plan (the daily-use document). Plus six specific Value Adds in the project tier, plus quarterly meetings and a year of support in the ongoing tier. Plus — and this matters — the confidence that comes from knowing your family’s plan is built, written down, and reviewed.
